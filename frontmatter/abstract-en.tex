\begin{englishabstract}
This study develops a reproducible procedure for evaluating the quality of responses generated by large language models. A single-inference setting is adopted to avoid the additional cost and time associated with repeated sampling. For each question, every experimental condition produces one response. Model identities are concealed with blind labels, and an LLM judge scores the responses using a five-dimension rubric. A subset of items is also reviewed by human evaluators to examine agreement between automated and human judgments.
